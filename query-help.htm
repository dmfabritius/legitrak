Searches are not case-sensitive and are composed of one or more of the following terms:
simple Either a single word, without spaces or punctuation; or a phrase enclosed in double quotes. For example, taxes or "tax code". Note that the phrase tax code without the quotes will cause an error.
prefix A word or phrase ending with an asterisk, enclosed in double-quotes. For example, "tax*" will find tax, taxes, taxation, etc. Note that tax* without the quotes will only find the word tax.
generational You can use the expression formsof(inflectional,word) to find variants of a given word. This applies to nouns, verbs, and adjectives. For example, formsof(inflectional,dry) would find dry, dried, and drying.
weighted The weighted keywords associated with any one of your tracking lists can be included in your search.

Search terms can be combined using the following operators:
and Finds documents containing all of the specified words or phrases. For example, peanut and butter finds documents with both the word peanut and the word butter.
or Finds documents containing at least one of the specified words or phrases. For example, peanut or butter finds documents containing either peanut or butter. The documents found could contain both items, but not necessarily.
and not Excludes documents containing the specified word or phrase. For example, peanut and not butter finds documents with peanut but not containing butter. The operator not must be used with the operator and. SQL Server does not accept peanut not butter; instead, specify peanut and not butter.
near Finds documents containing both specified words or phrases, and gives a higher relevance ranking to documents where the words are closer together. Note that the operator near is not supported in conjunction with the formsof search term; combining them will result in an error.
(  ) Use parentheses to group search terms. For example, "peanut butter" and (jelly or jam) finds documents with the words 'peanut butter with jelly' or 'peanut butter with jam' or both.
